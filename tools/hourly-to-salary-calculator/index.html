About this tool

Convert an hourly wage into its weekly, monthly, and annual equivalents based on how many hours you actually work per week. It is a quick way to compare an hourly offer against a salaried one or to sanity-check a contract rate.

How to use

  1. Enter your Hourly rate in dollars.
  2. Set Hours per week to your typical schedule (40 for full time, or a fraction for part time).
  3. Read the weekly, monthly, and annual pay from the results.

Frequently asked questions

How are the figures calculated?

Weekly pay is rate times hours; annual pay is weekly times 52; monthly pay is the annual figure divided by 12. So $35/hour at 40 hours is $1,400 weekly and $72,800 annually.

Does this account for taxes or unpaid time off?

No — it assumes 52 paid weeks and shows gross pay before taxes, benefits, or deductions. Subtract unpaid weeks from 52 and scale accordingly, or use the Take Home Pay Calculator for net figures.

Why is the monthly number not weekly pay times 4?

Months average about 4.33 weeks, so the tool divides annual pay by 12 instead of multiplying a week by 4, which would understate monthly income by roughly 8%.

Can I use it for overtime or part-time schedules?

Enter your average total weekly hours; the tool applies one flat rate, so blend overtime premiums into the rate manually if a large share of your hours are paid at time-and-a-half.

Is my wage information kept private?

Yes — the conversion happens instantly in your browser and nothing is transmitted or stored.
