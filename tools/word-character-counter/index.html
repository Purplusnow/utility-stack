About this tool

Get instant counts of words, characters, and sentences for any text you paste, plus an estimated reading time. Counts update as you type, making it easy to hit limits for tweets, meta descriptions, abstracts, or essays.

How to use

  1. Paste or type your content into the Text field.
  2. Read the Words, Characters, and Sentences metrics.
  3. Check the Reading time estimate to gauge length for your audience.

Frequently asked questions

How are words counted?

Text is split on runs of whitespace, so hyphenated terms count as one word and any spacing style (spaces, tabs, newlines) works the same.

Do spaces count as characters?

Yes. The character count is the total length of your text including spaces, punctuation, and line breaks, which matches how most platform limits are measured.

How is the sentence count derived?

It counts runs of sentence-ending punctuation (., !, ?). Abbreviations like "e.g." can inflate the number slightly, so treat it as an estimate.

What reading speed does the estimate assume?

225 words per minute, a common average for adult readers, rounded up to at least one minute. Use the Reading Time Calculator if you want a custom speed.

Is my text stored anywhere?

No. Counting happens in your browser as you type, with no uploads, logging, or storage.
