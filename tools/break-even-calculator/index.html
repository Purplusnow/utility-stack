About this tool

This calculator finds how many units you must sell to cover fixed costs, and how many more you need to hit a profit target. It computes contribution per unit and contribution margin, converts unit targets into revenue, and — given your conversion rate — into the number of leads or visitors required. A scenario table tests price, variable cost, and fixed cost changes, with a CSV export.

How to use

  1. Enter your monthly or project Fixed costs.
  2. Set the Price per unit and Variable cost per unit.
  3. Add a Target profit and your sales Conversion rate % to translate units into leads.
  4. Review break-even units, target units, revenue needed, and leads needed, then use Download break-even CSV for the scenarios.

Frequently asked questions

What formula determines break-even units?

Fixed costs divided by contribution per unit (price minus variable cost), rounded up to a whole unit. Target units use the same formula with the target profit added to fixed costs.

What is contribution margin?

The contribution per unit expressed as a percentage of price — the share of each sale left over to cover fixed costs after variable costs. A $50 price with $20 variable cost has a 60% contribution margin.

How are leads needed calculated?

Target units divided by your conversion rate, rounded up. At a 3% conversion rate, selling 250 units requires about 8,334 leads.

Why does the tool reject my inputs?

Price must be greater than zero and greater than the variable cost — if every sale loses money on variable costs alone, no volume can break even.

What do the scenario rows test?

Your baseline against price up 10%, variable cost up 10%, and fixed costs down 10%, showing how sensitive your break-even point is to each lever.

Is my cost data uploaded?

No — everything runs client-side in your browser, and these figures are planning estimates rather than accounting or financial advice.
